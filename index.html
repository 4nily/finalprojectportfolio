Project 1

Injected Ideals

Artist Statement

My piece addresses the beauty standards imposed by social media through the information we consume. I am trying to communicate with others who utilize social media platforms in their everyday lives to warn them and individuals who are intoxicated by comparison. My piece underscores the juxtaposition of oneself and others through the act of mindlessly scrolling on their phone. People consume and process information with expectations and beauty standards from social media to invent the perfect perception they want for themselves.

Project 2

Time Moves Along

Artist Statement

My video project is composed of captured moments from my digital camera during the 2025-2026 college year. Though the videos presented are primarily during the college year, the intended audience can be anyone. The narration over the video clips reflects themes of the time and creating fond memories, to convey the importance of being present while living and the minuscule moments missed when one is not paying attention to what is right in front of them.

The Data Behind Cinema

Artist Statement

My website displays data regarding the decades of cinema in a magazine style, combined with storytelling through coding languages such as HTML and CSS from scratch and graphs from Google Sheets, which I manually entered. I developed the concept and laid the foundation for the website because I am familiar with programming languages such as HTML. However, CSS is a language I personally struggle with to build from scratch, so I had to search on the internet through coding sites for how to make the appearance look like what I intended the magazine to look like and use Claude to explain what the code did for CSS, especially for the sidebar component of the magazine, which was harder than creating and coding the main content. I decided to go through with this project because I enjoy cinema and looking through statistics while showcasing my coding skills. I coded in Visual Studio Code and Dreamweaver with my mom's help to fix errors, since she can code as well, and the page is hosted on GitHub. The brief descriptions of the graphs in each section were originally written to explain what the charts displayed. The extras section links to real cinema magazines online and YouTube videos for anyone who is interested. As for the data, the references section lists the sources I consulted and estimated. I did not want to bore the viewer with charts, so making the website's aesthetic magazine-like was a decision to appeal to readers' eyes, and the final execution was what I had planned. My initial idea for this project was to create an interactive website; however, I later realized I’d rather code a website from scratch.
